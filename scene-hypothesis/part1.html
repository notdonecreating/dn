Part I

Foundations of The Scene Hypothesis

This part establishes the core concepts, characteristics, and mechanics of scenes as engines of progress. It explores what makes a scene different from other social groupings, how scenes drive innovation and progress, and what conditions are necessary for scenes to form.

The chapters in this section lay the theoretical groundwork for understanding scenes as a distinct social phenomenon with unique properties and dynamics. This framework will then be applied to development questions in subsequent parts.

Chapters in this section

Chapter 1

Understanding Scenes

Defines scenes and their core characteristics, exploring how they differ from formal organizations and function as magnetic centers of activity.
Chapter 2

Scene Anatomy & Life Cycle

Explores scenes as organisms with distinct anatomy and evolutionary patterns from birth through growth, maturity and eventual transformation.
Chapter 3

How Scenes Generate Progress

Examines mechanisms through which scenes foster innovation and creativity, from quantity-to-quality transformation to tacit knowledge transmission.
Chapter 4

Scene Prerequisites

Explores conditions necessary for scenes to form and flourish, including physical spaces, economic slack, and cultural permission.
